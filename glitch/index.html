MARC ABBEY • DAVID CIHELNA • RUBIN HUANG • YURIKA MULASE

Glitch the World is a live interactive storytelling experience about a young girl trying to break out of a digital world, losing her sense of reality along the way.

One of our goals with this project was to find an experience that would bridge gaming, interactive media, and film and create a novel, accessible way to participate in our narrative.

Our story takes place in a future city walled in from the natural world. The protagonist, Azra Dagney, is daughter of the creator and ruler, Richard Dagney, of their sterile city. In this city, citizens are protected by health-regulating biochips called Cubes and live in augmented realities that satisfy all of their daily needs and entertainment. One day, Azra receives a message from her brother, Barin, who had mysteriously disappeared from the city when she was younger. The message is a desperate request for Azra to leave the city center and find him outside the city limits.

The setup consists of a main screen and a smartphone for each audience member. Each device, including the screen, is networked locally to our servers, which we then control using Touch Designer. The experience is semi-automated - meaning that it's linear, but can be manipulated in real-time. The audience doesn’t need to download anything, just enter our URL into their preferred mobile browser upon entering. This reduces the barrier of entry to the experience, and doesn’t require additional equipment. The two main technologies we use are Node.js and Socket.io. Node.js is an asynchronous event driven framework that allows the creation of a server where many connections can be handled concurrently. Socket.io enables real-time bidirectional event-based communication across multiple devices.
